# Probability and Risk Experiments

Foundational notebooks for probability, statistics, trading risk, and simulation. These are the building blocks behind strategy evaluation, position sizing, drawdown awareness, and decision-making under uncertainty.

## Notebooks

| Notebook | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| [`01_probability_basics.ipynb`](01_probability_basics.ipynb) | Expected value, independent events, complement rule, binomial probability, conditional probability, Bayes theorem, variance, and standard deviation. |
| [`02_statistics_basics.ipynb`](02_statistics_basics.ipynb) | Mean, median, covariance, correlation, normal distribution, z-scores, confidence intervals, law of large numbers, and central limit theorem. |
| [`03_risk_metrics.ipynb`](03_risk_metrics.ipynb) | Maximum drawdown, Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio, Kelly criterion, and gambler's ruin. |
| [`04_simulations_and_monte_carlo.ipynb`](04_simulations_and_monte_carlo.ipynb) | Monte Carlo simulations, cumulative PnL paths, outcome distributions, drawdown behavior, and path dependency. |

## Why This Matters For Trading Research

- Expected value helps evaluate whether a setup is worth taking.
- Variance and drawdown help prevent overconfidence in average outcomes.
- Sharpe and Sortino add risk-adjusted context beyond raw return.
- Kelly sizing and gambler's ruin show why risk control matters even when an edge appears positive.
- Monte Carlo simulation helps stress-test trade sequences and understand worst-case paths.

## Notes

These notebooks are educational and research-oriented. Their purpose is to connect probability concepts to practical trading and market analysis, not to present formal academic research.
